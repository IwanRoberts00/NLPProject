<ICE-HK:W2D-002#1:1>
<bold> HOW TO APPLY FOR BIRTH REGISTRATION </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#2:1>
Under the relevant Ordinance , the birth of a child should be
registered at the registry of the district in which it occurred within a
period of 42 days .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#3:1>
A list of the births registries and the districts they serve is at the
end of this leaflet .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#4:1>
It would be preferable to attend the registry two weeks after the baby  ' s
birth in order to allow time for the birth records to reach the registry .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#5:1>
Where there is a genuine urgency for the birth to be registered
earlier , you may contact the officer-in-charge of the registry . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#6:1>
If the child was born before arrival at a hospital/clinic , the
birth should be registered at the Births and Deaths General Register Office
at 3rd Floor , Low Block , Queensway Government Offices , 66 Queensway , Hong
Kong . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#7:1>
The following original documents are required for the purpose of
birth registration :

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#8:1>
&obrack; a &cbrack; parents  '  marriage certificate; and

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#9:1>
&obrack; b &cbrack; &obrack; i &cbrack; parents  '  Hong Kong Permanent
Identity Cards; or &obrack; ii &cbrack; parents  '  identity cards and valid
travel documents &obrack; e.g. passports , documents of identity , etc.
&cbrack; . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#10:1>
Photocopies of the above documents are also required for record
purpose in the case where either parent is &lsquo; settled &rsquo; in Hong
Kong .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#11:1>
&lsquo; <mention> Settled </mention> &rsquo; means ordinarily
resident without being subject to any restriction in the period for which a
person may remain . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#12:1>
The attendance of either parent is sufficient .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#13:1>
In the case of an illegitimate child , the father  ' s name is to be
included in the register on the following circumstances :

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#14:1>
&obrack; a &cbrack; at the joint request of both parents; or

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#15:1>
&obrack; b &cbrack; at the request of the mother on production of
&obrack; i &cbrack; mother  ' s Declaration , and &obrack; ii &cbrack; father  ' s
Statutory Declaration to confirm the child  ' s paternity; or

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#16:1>
&obrack; c &cbrack; at the request of the father on production of
&obrack; i &cbrack; father  ' s Declaration , and &obrack; ii &cbrack; mother  ' s
Statutory Declaration to confirm the child  ' s paternity; or

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#17:1>
&obrack; d &cbrack; at the request of the father or mother on
production of &obrack; i &cbrack; a certified copy of relevant Court Order to
confirm the child  ' s paternity , and &obrack; ii &cbrack; if the child has
reached the age of 16 , the written consent of the child to the registration
of that person as his father . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#18:1>
No registration fee is payable if the birth is registered within
a period of 42 days .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#19:1>
A registration fee will be charged if the birth is registered after
the period of 42 days but within one year .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#20:1>
After one year , a birth can be registered only with the consent of
the Registrar of Births and Deaths and upon payment of a registration fee .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#21:1>
After registration of birth , it is advisable to obtain a certified
copy of birth entry on payment of a prescribed fee . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#22:1>
The capacity of birth registrations which can be handled by
district registries varies from one another depending on the district and
staff resources of each registry .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#23:1>
The existing capacities of respective registries are shown in this
leaflet . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#24:1>
<bold> Alteration/Addition to the Name of a Child </bold> </h> </p>
After registration of birth , the parent may apply at any of the births
registries to alter or add to the name of a child if desired .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#25:1>
No fee is payable if the alteration/addition to the name of a child
is made within the period of 42 days from the date of birth .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#26:1>
After such 42 days , a fee will be charged .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#27:1>
No alteration/addition to the name of a child shall be made after the
child has reached the age of 11 . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#28:1>
In applying for such alteration/addition to the name of a child ,
the parent must attend in person to make a declaration and produce the child  ' s
birth certificate .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#29:1>
A certified copy of birth entry with such alteration/addition to the
name of a child can be issued in 9 working days .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#30:1>
A fee will be charged for a certified copy of such entry . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#44:2>
<bold> HOW TO APPLY FOR DEATH REGISTRATION </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#45:2>
Under the relevant ordinance , deaths from natural causes and
where the deceased was attended by a registered medical practitioner must be
registered within 24 hours at the registry of the district in which it
occurred .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#46:2>
A list of the deaths registries and the districts they serve is shown
at the end of this leaflet . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#47:2>
Normally , if a person died of natural cause , the registered
medical practitioner who attended the deceased during his last illness will
sign and issue a Medical Certificate of the Cause of Death &obrack; Form 18
&cbrack; to the relative of the deceased for the purpose of death
registration .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#48:2>
The relative of the deceased , i.e. the informant , should bring
along the following documents for death registration :

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#49:2>
&obrack; a &cbrack; Medical Certificate of the Cause of Death
&obrack; Form 18 &cbrack;

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#50:2>
&obrack; b &cbrack; Deceased  ' s Hong Kong identity card/travel
document

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#51:2>
&obrack; c &cbrack; Informant  ' s Hong Kong identity card/travel
document </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#52:2>
The informant is required to furnish further information such as
the deceased  ' s occupation , nationality , marital status , etc. to the
Registrar for the purpose of registration .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#53:2>
Registration is free , however , after registration of death , a
certified copy of death entry can be obtained on payment of a prescribed fee .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#54:2>
For details of fees , please see the separate fee leaflet . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#55:2>
<bold> Unnatural Deaths Resulting from Sudden , Accidental or
Suspicious Causes </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#56:2>
In case where the death is resulted from unnatural causes
&obrack; e.g. poison , violence , etc. &cbrack; , such case will be reported
to the coroner .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#57:2>
The coroner may conduct a post-mortem examination or an inquest to
determine the cause of death .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#58:2>
Normally , it takes about one to six months for the coroner to
inquire into the cause of death .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#59:2>
When the cause of death is determined and made known by the coroner ,
the death will be registered within one week by the Registrar who will in
turn inform the deceased  ' s relative by letter .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#60:2>
All enquiries to such cases should be directed to the Births &ampersand; Deaths
General Register Office &obrack; Tel. 2867 2784 &cbrack; . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#61:2>
<bold> Permit for Removal of Dead Body from Hong Kong </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#62:2>
After registration of death , if the deceased  ' s body is to be
removed for burial outside Hong Kong , the applicant should apply for a
&lsquo; Permit for Removal of Dead Body from Hong Kong &rsquo; .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#63:2>
Applicant should complete an application form &obrack; SF/BDR/3
&cbrack; by giving information such as the deceased  ' s personal particulars ,
when and where the deceased  ' s body will be removed from Hong Kong , etc .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#64:2>
In some cases , the applicants may be required to produce evidence
that permission to import the dead body into a foreign country has been
obtained before the permit for removal of the dead body from Hong Kong is
issued .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#65:2>
No fee is payable for the issue of such permit . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#66:2>
<bold> Cremation of Dead Body </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#67:2>
Applications for cremation facilities are handled by relevant
offices of the Department of Health and Food and Environmental Hygiene
Department .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#68:2>
These offices are located at the joint office with &obrack; i
&cbrack; the Births and Deaths General Register Office &obrack; Hong Kong
Island Deaths Registry &cbrack; ; &obrack; ii &cbrack; the Births and Deaths
General Register Office &obrack; Kowloon Deaths Registry &cbrack; . </p> <h>

<I> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#86:3>
<bold> HOW TO APPLY FOR BUSINESS REGISTRATION </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#87:3>
Under the provisions of the Business Registration Ordinance ,
every person carrying on any business must register his business with the
Business Registration Office of the Inland Revenue Department .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#88:3>
Failure to do so may result in a fine of &dollar;5,000 and
imprisonment for one year . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#89:3>
&lsquo; <mention> Business </mention> &rsquo; means any form of
trade , commerce , craftsmanship , profession , calling or other activity
carried on for the purpose of gain and also means a club .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#90:3>
However , a person who is only holding an office or employment of
profit is not regarded as carrying on any business and is not required to
apply for business registration . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#91:3>
Every company incorporated in Hong Kong or to which Part XI of
the Companies Ordinance applies is deemed to be a person carrying on business
and is liable to be registered under the Business Registration Ordinance
whether or not it carries on business in Hong Kong .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#92:3>
The companies to which Part XI of the Companies Ordinance applies are
overseas companies which have established a place of business in Hong Kong .
</p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#93:3>
<bold> Business Exempt from Registration </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#94:3>
However , in view of certain exceptional cases , the Business
Registration Ordinance provides that the following businesses are exempted
from registration :

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#95:3>
a. the activities of charities &semi;

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#96:3>
b. the business of agriculture , market gardening or fishing &obrack;
exemption not applicable to Companies incorporated or required to be
registered under Part XI of the Companies Ordinance &cbrack; &semi;

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#97:3>
c. the business of a bootblack &semi;

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#98:3>
d. business carried on by such hawkers who require licences under the
Hawker Regulation , except businesses carried on inside the main structure of
a building . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#99:3>
<bold> When to Apply for Business Registration </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#100:3>
An application for business registration shall be made within
one month of the commencement of the business .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#101:3>
In case of a limited company , the application shall be made within
one month after date of incorporation or registration with the Companies
Registry . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#102:3>
All applications of registration of a business must be
accompanied by full payment of the appropriate business registration fee and
levy .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#103:3>
Currently the total amount of payment is &dollar;2,250 for a 1-year
Certificate and &dollar;5,950 for a 3-year Certificate . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#104:3>
<bold> Procedure for Business Registration </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#105:3>
The procedure for registering a business is simple .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#106:3>
All you have to do is to complete an appropriate application form
and return it to the Business Registration Office of the Inland Revenue
Department . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#107:3>
Forms may be obtained from the Receipt and Dispatch Centre of
the Inland Revenue Department in person or by post to the Business
Registration Office .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#108:3>
However , you should note that there are different application forms
for different businesses .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#109:3>
Applications should be made on : Form 1 &obrack; a &cbrack; , if the
business is carried on by an individual &semi; Form 1 &obrack; b &cbrack; ,
if the business is carried on by a body corporate &semi; Form 1 &obrack; c
&cbrack; , if the business is carried on by a partnership or by other body
unincorporate &semi; Form 1 &obrack; d &cbrack; , in the case of a branch .
</p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#110:3>
The forms are available free of charge .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#111:3>
If you do not know how to complete the form , you may seek advice
and assistance from the staff of the Enquiry Service Centre . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#112:3>
After completion , you may return the form to the Business
Registration Office by hand or by post together with the payment of business
registration fee and levy .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#113:3>
For registering a business carried on by a body corporate , a
photocopy of the certificate of incorporation should be forwarded with the
Form 1 &obrack; b &cbrack; .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#114:3>
For sole proprietorship and partnership , a photocopy of the Hong
Kong Identity Card/Passport of the proprietor or the partners should be
submitted with the completed application form &obrack; Form 1 &obrack; a
&cbrack; or Form 1 &obrack; c &cbrack; &cbrack; if the application is not
personally submitted . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#115:3>
<bold> Display of Certificate </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#116:3>
The valid business registration certificate must be displayed in
a conspicuous place at the address where the business is carried on and
should be produced for official inspection on demand . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#117:3>
<bold> Registration Fee </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#118:3>
Every person carrying on a business which has been registered is
required to pay an annual registration fee and levy of &dollar;2,250 with the
following exceptions :

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#119:3>
&obrack; 1 &cbrack; a business with turnover not exceeding
&dollar;30,000 monthly or

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#120:3>
&obrack; 2 &cbrack; a business with an income from the sale of
services not exceeding &dollar;10,000 monthly . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#121:3>
However , these exceptions are not applicable to a company
incorporated in Hong Kong or to which Part XI of the Companies Ordinance
applies .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#122:3>
Moreover , if two or more businesses are carried on by the same
proprietor or partners , none of those businesses is entitled to the
exemption .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#123:3>
An application for exemption in the case of a newly registered
business should be made to the Commissioner of Inland Revenue not later than
one month after the lodgement of the formal application for business
registration . Please note that payment of business registration fee and levy
upfront is still required even though an application for exemption from
payment is submitted together with the application for the registration of
that business at the same time .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#124:3>
In the case of an existing registered business , an application for
exemption for the following year should be made not later than one month
before the expiry of the current business registration certificate .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#125:3>
Application should be made on a Form 3 which can be obtained from
the Receipt &ampersand; Dispatch Centre of the Inland Revenue Department .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#126:3>
If it is approved , an exempt certificate valid for 12 months will
be issued . Exemption should be re-applied every year if applicable . </p>
<h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#127:3>
<bold> Branch Businesses </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#128:3>
If you carry on the same business at more than one address , you
must apply for the registration of branch business .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#129:3>
A valid branch registration certificate should be displayed at the
branch to which such certificate relates . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#130:3>
Branch registration certificate is issued on payment of
&dollar;323 for a 1-year Certificate and &dollar;939 for a 3-year Certificate .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#131:3>
The period of its validity is the same as that of the main
certificate .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#132:3>
If the main business has selected a 3-year Certificate , the branch
certificate will also be charged on a 3-year basis even though its
commencement date may not be the same as that of the main business . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#133:3>
<bold> Payment of Registration Fee </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#134:3>
Payment may be made by cheques , drafts or cashier orders
&obrack; made payable to &lsquo; The Government of the Hong Kong Special
Administrative Region &rsquo; and crossed &cbrack; .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#135:3>
Payment may also be made in cash at the Hong Kong Office , Kowloon
Sub-Office or Tsuen Wan Sub-Office of the Inland Revenue Department . </p>
<h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#136:3>
<bold> Change of Particulars </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-002#137:3>
If any change occurs in the particulars of your business as
stated on the application form for registration , you should report it to the
Business Registration Office .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#138:3>
Failure to do so may result in prosecution .

<ICE-HK:W2D-002#139:3>
Actually , the procedure is simple and no fee is charged . </I>
