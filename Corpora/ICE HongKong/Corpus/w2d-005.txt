<I> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#1:1>
<bold> HOW TO APPLY FOR DUTIABLE COMMODITIES PERMITS </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#2:1>
Any person who removes dutiable commodities is requi4red to have a
permit issued by the Customs and Excise Department under the Dutiable
Commodities Ordinance , Cap. 109. &obrack; Possession of a valid Import and
Export Licence for the dutiable commodities concerned is prerequisite for
permit application .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#3:1>
&cbrack; This leaflet outlines the circumstances under which permits
are necessary , the permit application procedure and the legal liabilities of
a permit holder . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#4:1>
<bold> What are Dutiable Commodities </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#5:1>
Dutiable Commodities Ordinance applies to the following four
categories of goods : <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#6:1>
&obrack; 1 &cbrack; alcoholic liquors &dash; </h> liquors such as
sheung ching , mow toi , ko leung , brandy , whisky , gin , rum , vodka ,
champagne , still wine , beer , cider and ethyl alcohol &semi; <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#7:1>
&obrack; 2 &cbrack; tobacco &dash; </h> tobacco products such as
cigarettes , cigars , smoking tobacco , unmanufactured leaf tobacco and
Chinese prepared tobacco , but not smokeless tobacco &semi; <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#8:1>
&obrack; 3 &cbrack; hydrocarbon oil &dash; </h> petroleum products
including motor spirit , aircraft spirit , light diesel oil and kerosene
&semi; <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#9:1>
&obrack; 4 &cbrack; methyl alcohol &dash; </h> a chemical for
industrial use only , commonly known as &lsquo; wood alcohol &rsquo; which
may cause death or loss of eyesight if drunk . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#10:1>
Dutiable commodities are the goods which are not exempt from duty
and on which the full duty prescribed by law has not been paid . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#11:1>
<bold> When is a Permit Required </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#12:1>
There are three types of permits :

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#13:1>
<it> &obrack; a &cbrack; Green Permit </it> or Removal Permit for
Dutiable Goods &obrack; Form CED 43L &cbrack; is required when &dash;
&obrack; i &cbrack; dutiable goods are removed from an importing conveyance
to a bonded warehouse &semi; &obrack; ii &cbrack; dutiable goods are removed
from a bonded warehouse to another bonded warehouse .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#14:1>
<it> &obrack; b &cbrack; Black Permit </it> or Removal Permit for
Duty-Paid Goods &obrack; Form CED 53L &cbrack; is required when &dash;
&obrack; i &cbrack; goods are removed for local use from the importing
conveyance or bonded warehouse upon payment of the full duty &semi; &obrack;
ii &cbrack; goods which are exempt from duty are removed from an importing
conveyance or a bonded warehouse &semi; &obrack; iii &cbrack; goods to which
the Dutiable Commodities Ordinance applies but attracting no duty are removed
from the importing conveyance for local use or to an exporting conveyance for
re-export &semi; &obrack; iv &cbrack; dutiable goods are removed from a
bonded warehouse to a place where the goods will be destroyed.

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#15:1>
&obrack; c &cbrack;<it> Red Permit </it> or Export Permit &obrack;
Form CED 82 &cbrack; is required when &dash; &obrack; i &cbrack; dutiable
goods are removed from a bonded warehouse to an exporting conveyance &semi;
&obrack; ii &cbrack; dutiable goods are removed from an importing conveyance
to an exporting conveyance for re-export . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#16:1>
<bold> How to Apply for a Permit </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#17:1>
Permit application forms may be obtained from the following
Customs and Excise Department offices :

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#18:1>
<it> &obrack; a &cbrack; </it> Hong Kong Permit Office 2/F , Harbour
Building 38 Pier Road Central Hong Kong

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#19:1>
<it> &obrack; b &cbrack; </it> Kowloon Permit Office 10/F , Canton
Road Government Offices 393 Canton Road Kowloon </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#20:1>
You should complete the application form in triplicate and submit
it , together with the necessary supporting documents such as invoice , bill
of lading , shipping order , air waybill , to any one of the above offices .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#21:1>
On all permit applications &obrack; CED 43L , CED 53L and CED 82
&cbrack; , you should include legibly the following particulars in the column
of Applicant  ' s Declaration :

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#22:1>
<it> &obrack; a &cbrack; </it> Name and position of the applicant who
signs on the relevant permits

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#23:1>
<it> &obrack; b &cbrack; </it> Company chop </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#24:1>
In order that the permit may be issued in time , it is important
that you provide all the information specified in the application form and
submit your application early .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#25:1>
You should also note that your permit application may be rejected if
the particulars of the licensee &obrack; licensee name , company name ,
company chop , address and telephone number &cbrack; entered thereon deviate
from those registered with the Customs and Excise Department . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#26:1>
On receipt of your application , the Permit Office will give you
a receipt and notify you of the date on which you should return to collect
the permit .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#27:1>
Normally , permits are ready for collection within two working days .
</p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#28:1>
<bold> How much is the Fee for a Permit </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#29:1>
Permit application forms are supplied free of charge and , apart
from the duty payable , no charge is made for the issue of permits .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#30:1>
If anyone approaches you for money or other advantages in connection
with the application , you should report to the Customs and Excise Department
, the ICAC or the police .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#31:1>
Offering money or advantages for any convenience in the processing of
a permit application is also an offence , and the person making the offer is
liable to prosecution . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#32:1>
<bold> What to do with a Permit </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#33:1>
You should present the permit , at the place where you take
delivery of the goods , to a customs officer &obrack; where no customs
officer is available , the person responsible for the goods , e.g. ship  ' s
officer if the goods are on board a vessel , or a warehouse-keeper if the
goods are in a warehouse &cbrack; for his endorsement .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#34:1>
The permit should then accompany the goods to their destination and ,
while the goods are being removed , should be produced on demand for
inspection by a member of the Customs and Excise Department or a police
officer .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#35:1>
Where the goods are removed under a &lsquo; green &rsquo; or &lsquo;
red &rsquo; permit , the permit should be presented at the destination of the
goods to a customs officer &obrack; where no customs officer is available ,
the person responsible for receiving the goods &cbrack; for his endorsement .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#36:1>
The duplicate of the permit should be returned to the Record and
Statistics Unit , Office of Dutiable Commodities Administration , 7/F ,
Harbour Building , 38 Pier Road , Central within seven working days from the
date of removal . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#37:1>
If for any reason particulars entered on a permit need to be
amended , you should make an application to the issuing office for this to be
done .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#38:1>
If a permit is not used , you should return it to the issuing office
for cancellation at once . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#39:1>
<bold> What are the Legal Liabilities of a Permit Holder </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#40:1>
A permit holder is liable for the duty payable on the goods
removed under the permit .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#41:1>
He is also liable to prosecution if the permit is used in
contravention of the Dutiable Commodities Ordinance , Cap. 109. </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#42:1>
You should not knowingly give incorrect information in a permit
application , alter or deface a permit .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#43:1>
Permits are not transferable and you should not use a permit issued
to another person or allow another person to use a permit issued to you .
</p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#44:1>
<bold> What are the Penalties for Breaching the Law in respect of
Permits </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#45:1>
A person who is convicted under the Dutiable Commodities
Ordinance may be punished with a fine of &dollar;1 million and imprisonment
for two years and , where an offence is committed with intent to avoid
payment of duty , an additional fine of 10 times the amount of duty payable .
</p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#46:1>

<I> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#50:2>
<bold> HOW TO APPLY FOR FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE UNDER THE LOCAL STUDENT
FINANCE SCHEME </bold> </h> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#51:2>
<bold> What is the Local Student Finance Scheme &obrack; LSFS
&cbrack; </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#52:2>
The LSFS is a means-tested scheme which provides financial
assistance to needy students studying eligible courses on a full-time basis
at the City University of Hong Kong , the Hong Kong Baptist University , the
Hong Kong Polytechnic University , the Lingnan University , the Chinese
University of Hong Kong , the Hong Kong University of Science &ampersand;
Technology , the University of Hong Kong , the Hong Kong Institute of
Vocational Education of the Vocational Training Council , the Prince Philip
Dental Hospital , the Hong Kong Institute of Education and the Hong Kong
Academy for Performing Arts . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#53:2>
The aim of the scheme is to ensure that no eligible students will
be denied access to one of the above institutions because of a lack of means .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#54:2>
Financial assistance is provided in the form of grant and/or loan .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#55:2>
The grant is to cover academic expenses , tuition fees and compulsory
students  '  union fees &semi; the loan is to cover living expenses . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#56:2>
<bold> Level of Financial Assistance </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#57:2>
The maximum amount of grant payable is equal to the actual
tuition fee and union fee payable and the academic expenses pertaining to the
course of study whilst the maximum amount of loan for living expenses is the
same for all students .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#58:2>
Applicants have to be income-tested .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#59:2>
The level of financial assistance , expressed as a percentage of the
maximum amount of grant and loan payable , is determined having regard to the
applicant  ' s family income as measured by its Adjusted Family Income per
household member .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#60:2>
Applicants with family assets &obrack; the value of the first home is
not counted &cbrack; exceeding certain pre-determined limits will either
become ineligible for any assistance or will have their assistance reduced .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#61:2>
The average amount of grant and loan offered in the 1999/2000
academic year were about &dollar;28,000 and &dollar;22,000 respectively .
</p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#62:2>
<bold> Who are Eligible </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#63:2>
Full-time students studying eligible courses in the institutions
covered by the Scheme who have right of abode in Hong Kong or have resided or
had their homes in Hong Kong continuously for three years immediately prior
to the commencement of their courses are eligible to apply . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#64:2>
<bold> When to Apply </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#65:2>
Applications are normally invited in April each year from current
students through announcement in the press and the institutions mentioned
above .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#66:2>
As for new students , they may apply when they are enrolled .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#67:2>
Application forms are obtainable from the institutions concerned .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#68:2>
Completed forms should be returned to the respective institutions
before the specified deadlines . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#69:2>
<bold> How to Complete the Application Form </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#70:2>
An applicant is required to furnish full particulars of his/her
family members , their income and assets together with relevant copies of
documentary proofs .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#71:2>
Such information will form the basis of determining the level of
financial assistance that he/she may obtain .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#72:2>
Any misrepresentation or omission may lead to disqualification and
court proceeding .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#73:2>
Applicants are reminded that it is an offence to obtain pecuniary
advantage by deception .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#74:2>
A person who does so commits an offence and is liable on conviction
to imprisonment for 10 years under the Theft Ordinance , Chapter 210 . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#75:2>
Guidance Notes are attached to each application form to assist
applicants in completing the form .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#76:2>
Students should study and follow the instructions carefully . </p>
<h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#77:2>
<bold> Payment Arrangement </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#78:2>
Notification of result will normally be issued within 2 months
from the date of receipt of the application which contains complete
information . Financial assistance is paid to the successful applicants in
two equal instalments .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#79:2>
Grant will be paid by direct credit into the student  ' s bank account
through the Government Treasury .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#80:2>
Loan will be paid in the same manner after completion and return of
the loan documents &obrack; see below &cbrack; . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#81:2>
A student who is successful in obtaining financial assistance in
one academic year does not qualify him/her for the same assistance in
subsequent years as each year  ' s application has to be assessed on the basis
of the prevailing financial circumstances of his/her family and the
assessment formula . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#82:2>
<bold> Requirements for Obtaining a Loan </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#83:2>
The following loan documents are required to be provided by a
successful applicant for the granting of loan :

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#84:2>
<it> &obrack; a &cbrack; </it> Undertaking &obrack; to be completed
by the applicant &cbrack; agreeing to repay the Government the full amount of
loan and interest upon graduation or ceassation of study &semi; and &obrack;
b &cbrack; Deed of Indemnity &obrack; to be completed by the indemnifier
&cbrack; agreeing to indemnify the Government against any loss resulting from
the applicant  ' s failure to honour the undertaking . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#85:2>
<bold> Repayment Arrangement </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#86:2>
Grants offered under the Scheme need not be repaid .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#87:2>
The loan recipients would be required to repay the loan with interest
at 2.5&percent; p.a. to the Government in twenty quarterly instalments within
a period of 5 years after he/she ceases to be a student at the institution on
or before completion of his/her course .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#88:2>
Interest will be charged and calculated from the commencement date of
the loan repayment period , which shall normally fall on 1 October of the
year of graduation .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#89:2>
The first quarterly repayment instalment of the loan will fall due on
1 January of the following year .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#90:2>
It must be noticed that if the payment of any instalment is delayed
by 7 days or more , a surcharge of 5&percent; on the amount of the overdue
instalment will be levied . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-005#91:2>
Deferment/suspension of loan repayment may be granted on grounds
of further studies , financial hardship or serious illness upon application
to the Student Financial Assistance Agency .

<ICE-HK:W2D-005#92:2>
All applications for deferment will be considered on individual
merits . </p> <h>
