<ICE-HK:W2D-007#1:1>
HOW TO APPLY FOR A LOTTERY LICENCE </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#2:1>
Anyone who wants to organize , conduct or promote a lottery in
Hong Kong has to apply for a lottery licence from the Commissioner for
Television and Entertainment Licensing . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#3:1>
Lottery includes: &bullet; a raffle &bullet; a sweepstake &bullet;
<indig> tse fa </indig> &bullet; <indig> hung piu </indig> &bullet; <indig>
po piu </indig> &bullet; any competition for money or other property success
in which involves guessing or estimating the results of future events , or of
past events , the results of which are not generally known; or does not
depend to a substantial degree upon the exercise of skill by the competitors;
and &bullet; any game , method , device or scheme for distributing or
allotting prizes by lot or chance , whether promoted , conducted or managed
in or outside Hong Kong . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#4:1>
Lottery licences are issued in accordance with the provisions of
the Gambling Ordinance and Regulations &obrack; Chapter 148 &cbrack; , copies
of which are on sale at the Government Publications Centre , G/F. , Low Block
, Queensway Government Offices , 66 Queensway , Hong Kong . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#5:1>
Where to Apply </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#6:1>
Application for a lottery licence should be made to the Television
and Entertainment Licensing Authority &obrack; the Licensing Authority
&cbrack; at Room 915 , 9/F. , Revenue Tower , 5 Gloucester Road , Wan Chai ,
Hong Kong &obrack; Tel : 2594 5811 &cbrack; , where application forms and
copies of the licence conditions are available .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#7:1>
Application forms are also available on the Internet &obrack;
http://www.info.gov.hk/forms/ &cbrack; . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#8:1>
Before completing the form &dash; in English or Chinese &dash;
please read the licence conditions and the related information carefully .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#9:1>
If you have any difficulties , the staff of the Licensing Authority
will be pleased to help . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#10:1>
The completed form should be sent to the Licensing Authority .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#11:1>
If the lottery involves tickets for sale , you should send two draft
sample tickets along with the application form .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#12:1>
The term &lsquo; <mention> lottery ticket </mention> &rsquo; includes
any document or thing or any tangible evidence showing the claim of any
person to participate in the lottery . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#13:1>
If you wish to apply for waiving of the licence fee , please also
forward to the Licensing Authority a letter giving reasons to support your
application . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#14:1>
You are advised not to get involved in any financial commitment
regarding the proposed lottery until the Licensing Authority has approved
your application for a lottery licence .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#15:1>
You could be wasting money if approval is withheld . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#16:1>
It normally takes two weeks for the Licensing Authority to
process an application .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#17:1>
If a licence is granted , a licence fee is payable unless the
Authority has agreed to waive it .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#18:1>
The current licence fee is &dollar;3,135 which is subject to revision
from time to time . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#19:1>
<h> When You Have a Licence </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#21:1>
After you have obtained a licence , the lottery tickets can be
printed .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#22:1>
Their content must be identical to the draft sample approved by the
Licensing Authority . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#23:1>
When you have published the lottery results in 2 local English
and 2 local Chinese newspapers on the date specified on the lottery tickets ,
a copy of the relevant newspaper cuttings should be sent to the Licensing
Authority . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#24:1>
It is also necessary to send to the Licensing Authority , within
28 days of the lottery draw , a statement of all moneys received from the
sale of the lottery tickets and every disbursement made from the moneys so
received . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#25:1>
Can I Apply for Change of the Particulars of the Lottery after I have
Obtained the Licence ? </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#26:1>
Yes .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#27:1>
If you need to change the particulars of your lottery &dash; such as
the date of the draw &dash; you are allowed to do so before the lottery
tickets are available for sale and you should explain to the Licensing
Authority in writing the reasons for the change required .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#28:1>
If permission is given , you will be requested to bring along the
licence to the Licensing Authority for amendment to be made upon payment of
an official signature fee .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#29:1>
The current fee is &dollar;140 which is subject to revision from time
to time . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#30:1>
What Should I do if I Wish to Sell Lottery Tickets in Public Place ?

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#31:1>
</h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#32:1>
If you wish to sell lottery tickets on any public streets , you
should quote your licence number and forward details of the plan to the
Licensing Authority at least 21 days beforehand for consideration and
approval .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#33:1>
You should also approach the Director of Lands or other authorities
for their agreement to the proposed use of the Government Land . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#34:1>
For the sale of lottery tickets at any other public places , such
as shopping centres , public housing estates , etc , permission is not
required from the Licensing Authority but you should obtain the consent of
the owner or other person with lawful authority to give such consent . </p>

<I> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#35:2>
HOW TO APPLY FOR A RADIO DEALERS LICENCE </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#36:2>
Under the provisions of the Telecommunication Ordinance &obrack;
Cap. 106 &cbrack; , any person in Hong Kong wishing to deal in the course of
trade or business in apparatus or material for radiocommunication or in any
component part of any kind that generates and emits radio waves shall need an
appropriate licence issued by the Telecommunications Authority . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#37:2>
However , no licence is required in respect of dealings in any
sound broadcast receiving apparatus and any television receiver .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#38:2>
Sound broadcast receiving apparatus means an apparatus which is
capable of receiving sound only , transmitted by means of radiocommunication
or by wire for reception by the general public .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#39:2>
Moreover , under the provisions of the Telecommunication &obrack;
Model Control Equipment &cbrack; &obrack; Exemption from Licensing &cbrack;
Order , the Telecommunication &obrack; Cordless Telecommunication Apparatus
&cbrack; &obrack; Exemption from Licensing &cbrack; Order and the
Telecommunication &obrack; Low Power Devices &cbrack; &obrack; Exemption from
Licensing &cbrack; Order , no person is required to obtain a licence from the
Telecommunications Authority to manufacture , possess , use or deal in model
control equipment , cordless telephone apparatus and low power device which
comply with all the technical specifications provided therein . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#40:2>
Licences and Licence Fees </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#41:2>
The following two kinds of Radio Dealers Licences are issued by
the Telecommunications Authority to companies properly registered in Hong
Kong :

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#42:2>
&obrack; i &cbrack; A Radio Dealers &obrack; Unrestricted &cbrack;
Licence legalises , so far as the Telecommunications Authority is concerned ,
a company so possess and deal in the course of trade or business in apparatus
or material for radio transmission and reception .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#43:2>
The licence fee is &dollar;1,500 per annum .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#44:2>
&obrack; ii &cbrack; A Radio Dealers &obrack; Restricted &cbrack;
Licence legalises , so far as the Telecommunications Authority is concerned ,
a company to possess and deal in the course of trade or business in apparatus
or material for radiocommunication other than transmitting apparatus or in
any component parts thereof .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#45:2>
The licence fee is &dollar;750 per annum . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#46:2>
Estimated Time Required to Obtain a Licence </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#47:2>
Subject to approval being granted by the Telecommunications
Authority , a licence will normally be issued within five working days after
the date of receipt of the requisite licence fee . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#48:2>
General Licence Conditions </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#49:2>
Licensees are bound legally to observe all the conditions
specified in the licences .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#50:2>
The principal ones are detailed as follows :

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#51:2>
&obrack; a &cbrack; The licensee shall carry on the business at the
licensed address only and shall not , without the consent of the
Telecommunications Authority , change such an address .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#52:2>
&obrack; b &cbrack; All the licensed apparatus shall be stored at the
licensed or a specified address and in no other place without the written
permission of the Telecommunications Authority .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#53:2>
&obrack; c &cbrack; The licensed apparatus shall not be used for or
by the licensee for the purpose of radiocommunication except under and in
accordance with an appropriate licence granted by the Telecommunications
Authority .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#54:2>
&obrack; d &cbrack; The licence is not transferable . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#55:2>
Penalty </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#56:2>
Any person who deals in apparatus or material for
radiocommunication &obrack; other than the items which are exempt from
licensing &cbrack; without an appropriate licence granted by the
Telecommunications Authority , shall be guilty of an offence .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#57:2>
The maximum penalty on summary conviction is a fine of &dollar;50,000
and imprisonment for two years . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#58:2>
Enquiries and Application Forms </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#59:2>
Information on the licensing requirement and application form can
be obtained via OFTA  ' s 24-hour Interactive Voice Response System &obrack;
Tel. 2891 0990 &cbrack; , OFTA  ' s Internet Home Page &obrack; Address :
http://www.ofta.gov.hk &cbrack; and OFTA  ' s Bulletin Board System &obrack;
Tel. 2834 0119 &cbrack; . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#60:2>
The Licensing Unit &obrack; Tel. 2961 6671 &cbrack; of the Office
of the Telecommunications Authority on 29/F , Wu Chung House , 213 Queen  ' s
Road East , Wan Chai , Hong Kong , will gladly give further advice upon
request . </p> </I>

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#61:3>
HOW TO APPLY FOR A MODEL CONTROL LICENCE </h> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#62:3>
Definition of a Model Control Equipment </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#63:3>
A Model Control Equipment is defined as a radiocommunication
apparatus designed or adapted for the purpose of controlling &obrack; other
than by means of telephony &cbrack; the movement of a model vehicle , vessel
or aircraft or a model of any other type by means of the emission of
electromagnetic energy from that apparatus and the reception of such energy
by the receiving apparatus in the model . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#64:3>
Reason for Licensing of Model Control Equipment </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#65:3>
A frequency band , 26.96 MHz to 27.28 MHz , and two other
frequencies , 72.08 MHz and 72.96 MHz , have been assigned for the operation
of model control equipment in Hong Kong .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#66:3>
Without placing some limitation on the technical performance of the
device operating on these frequencies , harmful interference may be caused to
other authorised users of the frequency spectrum .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#67:3>
Therefore , the government needs to exercise control by licensing .
</p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#68:3>
The Telecommunication Ordinance &obrack; Cap. 106 &cbrack; and
its Telecommunication Regulations provide that any person in Hong Kong
wishing to possess , trade , establish and maintain and use a model control
equipment , is required to obtain a Model Control Licence issued by the
Telecommunications Authority . However , with effect from 1981 , a large
number of model control equipment have been exempted by law from licensing

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#69:3>
&obrack; See paragraph below &cbrack; . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#70:3>
Model Control Equipment Which Are Exempt from Licensing </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#71:3>
The Telecommunication &obrack; Model Control Equipment &cbrack;
&obrack; Exemption from Licensing &cbrack; Order 1981 provides that no person
in Hong Kong shall be required to apply for or hold a licence granted by the
Telecommunications Authority to cover the possession , use or dealings in
model control equipment if they comply with ALL the following conditions:

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#72:3>
A. The operating frequency shall be within the frequency band 26.96
MHz to 27.28 MHz .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#73:3>
B. The operating radio frequency determining element is sealed so
that it cannot be adjusted .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#74:3>
C. The maximum mean power of the emission does not exeed 0.5 watt .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#75:3>
D. Emission is confined within a 20 kHz band centred on the carrier
frequency .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#76:3>
The strength of the electric field of the spurious emission produced
by the model control equipment shall not exceed 500 &mu;V/m measured at a
distance of three metres from the model control equipment . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#77:3>
It is important to note that although exempted from licensing ,
any person using model control equipment specified in the above paragraph is
legally bound to comply with the following conditions:

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#78:3>
A. The equipment must be made available for inspection , if so
required , by duly authorised officers of the Telecommunications Authority .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#79:3>
B. The equipment shall not be caused undue interference with any
other authorised telecommunication apparatus or system .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#80:3>
C. Any direction given by the authority for the purpose of avoiding
such interference must be observed . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#81:3>
The Model Control Licence and Licence fee </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#82:3>
All owners &obrack; other than licensed radio dealers &cbrack; of
a licensable model control equipment are required to obtain a Model Control
Licence from the Office of the Telecommunications Authority at 29/F. , Wu
Chung House , 213 Queen  ' s Road East , Wan Chai , Hong Kong .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#83:3>
The transmitting apparatus &obrack; with batteries &cbrack; and the
Hong Kong Identity Card or passport of the applicant should be made available
for inspection .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#84:3>
The annual fee for this licence is &dollar;55 . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#85:3>
A licence would normally be issued within 14 days after the date
of receipt of the requisite licence fee . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#86:3>
Licence Conditions </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#87:3>
Licensees are bound legally to observe all the conditions
specified in the licence .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#88:3>
The principal ones are detailed as follows :

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#89:3>
A. The licensed apparatus shall be operated only &obrack; i &cbrack;
by the licensee personally or &obrack; ii &cbrack; in the presence of and
under the direct supervision of the licensee , by any other person authorised
by him .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#90:3>
B. The apparatus shall not cause any interference with any other duly
licensed or authorized telecommunications .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#91:3>
C. A satisfactory method of frequency stabilization shall be employed
in the transmitting apparatus .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#92:3>
D. The apparatus and the licence shall be available for inspection at
all reasonable times by duly authorized officers of the Telecommunications
Authority .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#93:3>
E. The licence is not transferable . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#94:3>
Penalty </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-007#95:3>
Any person who trades in the course of business , possesses or
uses a model control equipment &obrack; other than the types which are exempt
from licensing &cbrack; without an appropriate licence granted by the
Telecommunications Authority shall be guilty of an offence .

<ICE-HK:W2D-007#96:3>
The maximum penalty on summary conviction is a fine of &dollar;50,000
and imprisonment for two years . </p> <h>
