<I> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#1:1>
<bold> HOW TO APPLY FOR PERMIT TO USE &ampersand; OPERATE A KIDDIE
RIDE </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-006#2:1>
The purpose of permit system is to regulate all kiddie rides
operating in Hong Kong for use by the public in accordance with the safety
requirements set out in the Amusement Rides &obrack; Safety &cbrack;
Ordinance , Cap.449 and the Amusement Rides &obrack; Safety &cbrack; &obrack;
Operation and Maintenance &cbrack; Regulation. </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#3:1>
<bold> Interpretation of kiddie rides and to whom it may concern
</bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-006#4:1>
&ldquo; <mention> Kiddie ride </mention> &ldquo; means an
amusement ride designed primarily for use by children aged not more than 12
years; and having &obrack; a &cbrack; a designed carrying capacity of not
more than 100 Kg; and &obrack; b &cbrack; a motive power of not more than 1.1
kW . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-006#5:1>
The owner of a kiddie ride , either the licensee under the Place
of Public Entertainment Ordinance &obrack; Cap. 172 &cbrack; or Amusement
Game Centre &obrack; Cap. 435 &cbrack; , a lessee or a hirer of the ride ,
shall obtain a permit from Electrical and Mechanical Services Department
&obrack; EMSD &cbrack; before operating the ride for use by members of the
public . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#6:1>
<bold> How to apply </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-006#7:1>
Application for the permit to use and operate a kiddie ride should
be made to the General Legislation Sub-division of EMSD .

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#8:1>
The procedures for application are as follows . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-006#9:1>
&obrack; a &cbrack; Obtain an application form &obrack; 1 &cbrack;
by either of the following ways : &dash;

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#10:1>
i. in person to the General Legislation Sub-division , EMSD , Room
407B , 98 Caroline Hill Road , Causeway Bay , Hong Kong during office hours :
Weekdays 09:00 to 16:30 Saturdays 09:00 to 12:00

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#11:1>
On Sunday and public holiday , the office will be closed .

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#12:1>
ii. in writing to the General Legislation Sub-division of EMSD
stating the requisition of application form <fnr> &obrack; 1 &cbrack; </fnr>
, together with a stamped and self-addressed envelope .

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#13:1>
&obrack; b &cbrack; Complete the application form in accordance with
the Guidance Notes and appoint a Surveyor or Qualified Person for examination
on the ride .

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#14:1>
&obrack; c &cbrack; Submit the completed application form together
with the supporting documents , ride examination report and the prescribed
application fee <fnr> &obrack; 2 &cbrack; </fnr> either i. in person or by
post to the General Legislation Sub-division of EMSD; or ii. via the e-mail
submission@emsd.gov.hk . <fnr> &obrack; 3 &cbrack; </fnr>

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#15:1>
&obrack; d &cbrack; The supporting documents above mentioned include
: &dash; i. Photograph of the kiddie ride . ii. A location plan of the
premises in question and a drawing showing dimensions of kiddie ride and the
clearance from nearby objects . iii. A sample of notice of prohibited conduct
for the attention of the riders .

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#16:1>
&obrack; e &cbrack; Upon receipt of the submission , a site
inspection will be arranged and the applicant may be required to explain and
clarify the design and construction of the kiddie ride to EMSD  ' s officers
and supplement further information if necessary . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#17:1>
<bold> Notification of result </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-006#18:1>
A Form 6 titled &ldquo; Approval to commencement of operation of
kiddie ride &rdquo; will be issued to the applicant for retention if the
application is successful .

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#19:1>
A ride location number , stated in the Form 6 , will be assigned to
the ride for future reference . </p> <h>

Enquiry </h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#29:2>
<bold> HOW TO APPLY FOR PERMIT TO USE &ampersand; OPERATE AMUSEMENT
RIDES </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-006#30:2>
The purpose of permit system is to regulate all amusement rides
operating in Hong Kong for use by the public in accordance with the safety
requirements set out in the Amusement Rides &obrack; Safety &cbrack;
Ordinance , Cap. 449 , <bold> ARSO </bold> and the Amusement Rides &obrack;
Safety &cbrack; &obrack; Operation and Maintenance &cbrack; Regulation ,
<bold> AR &obrack; S &cbrack; &obrack; O&M &cbrack; R </bold> . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#31:2>
<bold> Scope and to whom it may concern </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-006#32:2>
Pursuant to section 6 and 10 of ARSO , owner of amusement ride
shall obtain a design approval before equipment installation and thereafter a
permit to use and operate from Electrical and Mechanical Services Department
&obrack; EMSD &cbrack; before operating any amusement ride for use by members
of the public .

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#33:2>
This leaflet is not applicable to the application for kiddie ride
which however is covered in another leaflet under the same series of
publicity materials for ARSO . </p> <h>

407B , 98 Caroline Hill Road , Causeway Bay , Hong Kong during office hours :

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#37:2>
Weekdays 09:00 to 16:30

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#38:2>
Saturdays 09:00 to 12:00

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#39:2>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-006#40:2>

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#41:2>

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#93:3>
<bold> HOW TO APPLY FOR A PLEASURE VESSEL RADIO NETWORK STATION
LICENCE </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-006#94:3>
In Hong Kong , the establishment or maintenance of any
radiocommunication and/or radio navigational apparatus on board a pleasure
vessel or yacht licensed in Hong Kong or in yacht clubs/marinas is subject to
control under the Telecommunication Ordinance , Cap. 106 .

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#95:3>
If the vessel owner or the club manager wishes to establish a radio
station on board vessel or in the yacht club , he is required to take out a
Pleasure Vessel Radio Network Station Licence &obrack; PVRNS Lic. &cbrack;
from the Office of the Telecommunications Authority &obrack; OFTA &cbrack; .
</p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#96:3>
The Use of Pleasure Vessel Radio Network </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-006#97:3>
The Pleasure Vessel Radio Network is a local radio network for
Hong Kong-based pleasure vessels or yachts fitted with radio equipment to
communicate with the following parties : &obrack; 1 &cbrack; other pleasure
vessels fitted with similarly licensed radio installations; &obrack; 2
&cbrack; yacht clubs , marinas , etc. fitted with similarly licensed radio
stations; and &obrack; 3 &cbrack; telephone subscribers ashore through the
Hong Kong Coast Station operated by the Hong Kong Telecom International Ltd
</p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#98:3>
<bold> General Licensing Conditions </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-006#99:3>
&obrack; 1 &cbrack; For installation on board pleasure vessels:
&obrack; a &cbrack; Licence will only be issued for radio installation on
board vessels which are licensed by the Director of Marine , Hong Kong , as
pleasure vessels or sailing yachts .

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#100:3>
&obrack; b &cbrack; The licence would be issued in the name of the
licensed owner of the pleasure vessel .

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#101:3>
&obrack; c &cbrack; For stations authorised to use
channels/frequencies specified for working with the Hong Kong Coast Station ,
a maritime accounting authority which has been recognised by the
Telecommunications Authority must be appointed to settle the radio account
for traffic of the vessel .

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#102:3>
&obrack; List of recognised accounting authorities is available on
request . &cbrack; </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-006#103:3>
&obrack; 2 &cbrack; For shore-based installation in yacht club ,
marina , etc. : &obrack; a &cbrack; These shore-based stations will be
authorised to use only channels 16 , 96 and 99 on the strict understanding
that channel 16 , being a distress channel , must not be used except for
communications involving safety of life at sea .

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#104:3>
&obrack; b &cbrack; Licence will be issued in the registered name of
the club or marina . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#105:3>
<bold> Radio And Electronic Navigational Apparatus Installed </bold>
</h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-006#106:3>
The radio equipment installed &obrack; e.g. HF SSB or VHF
transceiver , radar , EPIRB , etc. &cbrack; shall be type approved to either
Hong Kong Telecommunications Authority specifications &obrack; i.e.
specifications HKTA 1001 for HF SSB and HKTA1005 for VHF transceiver &cbrack;
or relevant International Maritime Organisation performance specifications .
</p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-006#107:3>
Moreover , the operating frequency of radar shall be within the
band of 2920-3266 MHz or 9300-9500 MHz and within the limits defined in the
ITU Radio Regulations . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-006#108:3>
In addition , the operating frequency of EPIRB shall be either
121.5MHz, 121.5/243MHz or 406MHz .

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#109:3>
The EPIRB must be provided with built-in facilities to enable its
performance to be tested without radiation of radio wave energy .

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#110:3>
The ship  ' s name , the call sign and the word &ldquo; Hong Kong
&rdquo; must be indelibly painted on the exterior of the equipment for
identification . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#111:3>
<bold> Radiotelephone Operator </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-006#112:3>
&obrack; 1 &cbrack; The minimum qualifications are : &obrack; a
&cbrack; For VHF installation , a Restricted Certificate of Competency in
Radiotelephony &obrack; Hong Kong Pleasure Vessel &cbrack; endorsed for VHF
operation only .

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#113:3>
&obrack; b &cbrack; For installation with both VHF and HF &obrack;
SSB &cbrack; equipment , a Restricted Certificate of Competency in
Radiotelephony &obrack; Hong Kong Pleasure Vessel &cbrack; . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-006#114:3>
&obrack; 2 &cbrack; Except in case of distress or as provided in
para. &obrack; 3 &cbrack; &ampersand; &obrack; 4 &cbrack; below, the station
must be operated by a person holding an appropriate radio operator
certificate issued &obrack; or recognised &cbrack; by the Telecommunications
Authority . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-006#115:3>
&obrack; 3 &cbrack; For the use of channels 96 and 99 &obrack;
i.e. private channels for local pleasure vessel radio network &cbrack; only ,
the radio operator qualification is not required . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-006#116:3>
&obrack; 4 &cbrack; Certificates of similar categories issued by
some other countries are recognised as equivalent to the certificates issued
by the Telecommunications Authority .

<ICE-HK:W2D-006#117:3>
Details are available from OFTA . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-006#118:3>
&obrack; 5 &cbrack; Any person wishing to sit examinations for
the issue of the above certificates must apply to the Maritime Services
Sub-section , Office of the Telecommunications Authority . </p> <h>
