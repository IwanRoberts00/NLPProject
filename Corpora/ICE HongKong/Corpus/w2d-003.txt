<I> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#1:1>
<bold> NOTES TO APPLICANTS FOR ONE-OFF GRANT TO DIESEL TAXI OWNERS TO
REPLACE THEIR VEHICLES WITH LIQUEFIED PETROLEUM GAS &obrack; LPG &cbrack;
TAXIS </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#2:1>
To improve the air quality in Hong Kong , the Government is
actively considering a gradual replacement of diesel vehicles with vehicles
using cleaner fuels .

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#3:1>
The replacement of diesel taxis with LPG taxis is one of the measures
to achieve this objective . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#4:1>
In order to encourage an early switch to LPG taxis by owners of
diesel taxis , the Government will provide a one-off grant to each eligible
diesel taxi owner for replacing his/her diesel taxi with an LPG taxi .

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#5:1>
The grant for replacement of a diesel taxi is &dollar;40,000 .

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#6:1>
Applications for the grant will be invited as from 8 August 2000
onwards . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#7:1>
<bold> Eligibility and Amount of Grant </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#8:1>
&obrack; 1 &cbrack; The first registered owner &obrack; Note 1
&cbrack; of an LPG taxi that was first registered on or after 7 October 1999
for the replacement of his/her diesel taxi is eligible to apply for the full
grant of &dollar;40,000 . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#9:1>
&obrack; Note 1 : The first registered owner refers to the first
registered owner of the LPG taxi stated in the Vehicle Registration Document
issued by the Transport Department in respect of that LPG taxi . &cbrack;
</p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#10:1>
&obrack; 2 &cbrack; The first registered owner of an LPG taxi
that was first registered on or before 6 October 1999 for the replacement of
his/her diesel taxi is eligible to apply for 50&percent; of the grant , i.e.
&dollar;20,000 .

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#11:1>
The current registered owner &obrack; Note 2 &cbrack; of the LPG taxi
is also eligible to apply for 50&percent; of the grant , i.e. &dollar;20,000 .
</p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#12:1>
&obrack; Note 2 : The current registered owner refers to the
registered owner of the LPG taxi in accordance with the record as at the
close of business of the Licensing Division , Transport Department on 7
August 2000 . &cbrack; </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#13:1>
<bold> Deadline for Application </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#14:1>
&bullet; All diesel taxi owners who replace their diesel taxis
with LPG taxis on or before 31 December 2001 can apply for the grant during
the period from 8 August 2000 to 31 December 2001 .

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#15:1>
Diesel taxi owners whose diesel taxis aged seven years or above
&obrack; Note 3 &cbrack; on or before 31 December 2001 must apply for the
grant on or before 31 December 2001 for replacing their diesel taxis with LPG
taxis . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#16:1>
&bullet; Diesel taxi owners whose diesel taxis aged six years or
below &obrack; Note 3 &cbrack; during the period from 1 January 2002 to 31
December 2003 can still apply for the grant during the same period for
replacing their diesel taxis with LPG taxis . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#17:1>
&obrack; Note 3 : The age of a diesel taxi is calculated in
accordance with the date of first registration . &cbrack; </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#18:1>
<bold> Method and Procedure of Application </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#19:1>
&bullet; The first registered owner of an LPG taxi that was first
registered on or after 7 October 1999 should complete application form TD537 .
</p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#20:1>
&bullet; The first and the current owners of an LPG taxi that was
first registered on or before 6 October 1999 should complete application form
TD536 . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#21:1>
&bullet; Application forms are available at the following
locations :

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#22:1>
&bullet; The following Licensing Offices of the Transport Department
:

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#23:1>
1. 3/F. , United Centre , 95 Queensway , Hong Kong

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#24:1>

, Sham Shui Po , Kowloon

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#25:1>
3. 5/F. , Kowloon East Government Offices , 12 Lei Yue Mun Road ,
Kwun Tong , Kowloon

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#26:1>
4. 5/F. , City Link Plaza , Shatin Station , New Territories

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#27:1>
&bullet; The following Vehicle Examination Centres of the Transport
Department :

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#28:1>
1. Kowloon Bay Centre , 2 Cheung Yip Street , Kowloon

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#29:1>

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#30:1>
3. Sheung Kwai Chung Centre , 302 Woo Yee Hop Road , Kwai Chung , New

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#31:1>
&bullet; The home page of the Transport Department &obrack;
http://www.info.gov.hk/td &cbrack;

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#32:1>
&bullet; District Offices </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#33:1>
&bullet; The following documents must be produced upon submission
of the application form :

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#34:1>
&obrack; 1 &cbrack; Photocopy of the applicant  ' s Hong Kong Identity
Card or identity document &obrack; s &cbrack; , or the relevant Certificate
of Incorporation in the event that the applicant is a limited company .

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#35:1>
Business Registration Certificate will not be accepted .

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#36:1>
&obrack; 2 &cbrack; The applicant who is also the current registered
owner of the LPG taxi will need to produce the original Vehicle Registration
Document or the true copy of the Vehicle Registration Document certified by a
legal practitioner , certified public accountant or financial institution .
</p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#37:1>
&bullet; As from 8 August 2000 onwards , those owners of the
diesel taxis who have completed the procedure for changing to LPG taxis can
submit the completed application forms in person or by an agent authorized in
writing , together with the documents mentioned in &obrack; 1 &cbrack; or
&obrack; 2 &cbrack; above to the Public Vehicle Section of the Transport
Department at 3/F. , United Centre , 95 Queensway , Hong Kong during office
hours &obrack; Mondays to Fridays : 9:00 a.m. to 1:00 p.m. and 2:00
p.m. to 5:00 p.m. &semi; Saturdays : 9:00 a.m. to 12.00 noon &cbrack; .
</p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#38:1>
&bullet; Those diesel taxi owners who is in the process but yet
to complete the procedure for replacing their diesel taxis with LPG taxis can
submit their application forms together with the application for
de-registration of their diesel taxis &obrack; TD184 or TD129 &cbrack; . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#39:1>
&bullet; All applicants are required to provide their personal
particulars and the information about their diesel and LPG taxis in the
application form as far as possible .

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#40:1>
The information required will include that relates to the vehicle
registration mark and the date of de-registration of their diesel taxis as
well as the vehicle registration mark and the date of first registration of
their LPG taxis . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#41:1>
Collection of Grant </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#42:1>
&bullet; The taxi owner may choose to collect the payment of
grant by one of the following methods : &obrack; 1 &cbrack; A crossed cheque
to be sent by mail to a specified correspondence address &semi; &obrack; 2
&cbrack; A crossed cheque to be collected from the Hong Kong Licensing Office
of the Transport Department , 3/F. , United Centre , 95 Queensway &semi;
&obrack; 3 &cbrack; A crossed cheque to be collected from Central
Sub-Treasury , 1/F. , Central Government Offices , West Wing , 11 Ice House
Street , Central &semi; &obrack; 4 &cbrack; The grant to be credited directly
into a specified bank account &obrack; GF179A &ldquo; Authority for payment
to a bank &rdquo; must be duly completed and submitted together with the
application form &cbrack; . </p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#43:1>
<bold> <it> Other related questions </it> </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#44:1>
<bold> Can the payment of grant be directed to a person or a
company other than the taxi owner i.e. the applicant ? </bold> &bullet; No .

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#45:1>
The payee on the crossed cheque must be the applicant himself/herself
, i.e. the registered owner of the relevant taxi .

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#46:1>
If the applicant chooses to have the payment credited to a specified
bank account , the holder of that bank account must be the applicant
himself/herself . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#47:1>
<bold> What documents should be produced if the applicant chooses
to collect cheque from the Transport Department or the Treasury ? </bold>
&bullet; For cheques to be collected from the Hong Kong Licensing Office of
the Transport Department : &bullet; The applicant who has completed the
procedure for replacing his/her diesel taxi on or before 7 August 2000 should
produce the original copy of the written notification issued to him/her
through mail or fax by the Transport Department , and his/her Hong Kong
Identity Card .

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#48:1>
In the event that the applicant is a limited company , a written
authorization of that company bearing the same company chop and signature as
shown on the application form must be produced . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#49:1>
&bullet; The applicant who is still in the process of replacing
his/her diesel taxi on or after 8 August 2000 should produce the original
copy of the written acknowledgement of receipt of application issued to
him/her by the Transport Department , and his/her Hong Kong Identity Card .

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#50:1>
In the event that the applicant is a limited company , a written
authorization of the company bearing the same company chop and signature as
shown on the application form must be produced . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#51:1>
&bullet; For cheques to be collected from the Central
Sub-Treasury : &bullet; The applicant should produce the original copy of the
remittance advice issued by the Treasury and his/her Hong Kong Identity Card .

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#52:1>
In the case that the applicant is a limited company , an official
receipt issued by that company must be produced . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#53:1>
<bold> Can an agent be appointed to collect the cheque on behalf
of the applicant ? </bold> Yes . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#54:1>
&bullet; For cheques to be collected from the Hong Kong Licensing
Office of the Transport Department : &bullet; For those applicants who have
completed the procedure for replacing their diesel taxis on or before 7
August 2000 , the agent of a particular applicant must produce his/her Hong
Kong Identity Card , an authorization letter signed by the applicant , a
photocopy of the applicant  ' s Hong Kong Identity Card and the original copy
of the written notification sent to the applicant by mail or fax by the
Transport Department .

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#55:1>
In the event that the applicant is a limited company , the
authorization should bear the same company chop and signature as shown on the
application . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#56:1>
&bullet; For those applicants who are still in the process of
replacing their diesel taxis on or after 8 August 2000 , the agent of a
particular applicant must produce his/her Hong Kong Identity Card , an
authorization letter signed by the applicant , a photocopy of the applicant  ' s
Hong Kong Identity Card and the original copy of the written acknowledgement
of receipt of application issued by the Transport Department .

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#57:1>
In the event that the applicant is a limited company , that
authorization should bear the same company chop and signature as those on the
application . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#58:1>
&bullet; For cheques to be collected from the Central
Sub-Treasury : &bullet; The agent should produce his/her Hong Kong Identity
Card , an authorization letter signed by the applicant , a photocopy of the
applicant  ' s Identity Card and the original copy of the remittance advice
issued by the Treasury .

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#59:1>
In the event that the applicant is a limited company , an official
receipt issued by the company must also be produced . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#60:1>
<bold> Can an agent be appointed to amend or add any information
on an application form that has already been submitted ? </bold> &bullet; No .

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#61:1>
An applicant who wishes to amend or add any information on the
application form must do so by himself/herself . </p>

<p> <h>

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#62:1>
<bold> Time required for collection of payment of grant </bold> </h>
&bullet; Applicants who have completed the procedure for replacing their
diesel taxis on or before 7 August 2000 may be able to collect the grant in
two weeks upon submission of the applications . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#63:1>
&bullet; Applicants who have completed the procedure for
replacing their diesel taxis on or after 8 August 2000 and who have chosen to
collect cheques from the Hong Kong Licensing Office of the Transport
Department may be able to collect the payment within the next working day
upon submission of the applications.

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#64:1>
Applicants who have chosen to collect their payment through other
methods may collect the grant within one week .

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#65:1>
The Transport Department is committed to trying its best to issue the
grant within the above time-frame .

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#66:1>
However , in case where voluminous applications are received at one
time , a longer processing time will be needed to handle the applications and
hence the issue of payment may be delayed . </p>

<p> 
<ICE-HK:W2D-003#67:1>
<bold> Can an applicant lodge an appeal in case his/her
application is rejected ? </bold>

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#68:1>
&bullet; Yes .

<ICE-HK:W2D-003#69:1>
The applicant may lodge an appeal in writing with the Transport
Department within one month from the date of issue of the notification of the
application result . </p> <h>
