<ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#2:1> <h> Item 1. Business</h>

<p> <ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#3:1> The Registrant, incorporated in December 1925, serves business,

professional and educational markets around the world with information

products and services.
<ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#4:1> Key markets include finance, business, education, law, construction,

medical and health, computers and communications, aerospace and defense.
<ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#5:1> As a multimedia publishing and information company, the Registrant

employs a broad range of media, including books, magazines, newsletters,

software, on-line data services, CD-ROMs, facsimile and television

broadcasting.
<ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#6:1> Most of the Registrant 's products and services face substantial

competition from a variety of sources.</p>

<p> <ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#7:1> The Registrant 's 15,004 employees are located worldwide.
<ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#8:1> They perform the vital functions of analyzing the nature of changing

demands for information and of channeling the resources necessary to fill

those demands.
<ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#9:1> By virtue of the numerous copyrights and licensing, trade, and other

agreements, which are essential to such a business, the Registrant is able to

collect, compile, and disseminate this information.
<ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#10:1> Substantially all book manufacturing and magazine printing is handled

through a number of independent contractors.
<ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#11:1> The Registrant 's principal raw material is paper, and the Registrant

has assured sources of supply, at competitive prices, adequate for its

business needs.</p>

and markets, and significant achievements are hereby incorporated by

reference from Exhibit ( 13), pages 4 through 24 ( textual material) of the

Registrant 's 1995 Annual Report to Shareholders.</p>

<ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#15:1> <h> Item 2. Properties</h>

<p> <ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#16:1> The Registrant leases office facilities at 393 locations, 294 are

in the United States.
<ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#17:1> In addition, the Registrant owns real property at 25 locations; 22

are in the United States.
<ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#18:1> The principal facilities of the Registrant are as follows:</p>

<p> <ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#19:1> The Registrant 's major lease covers space in its headquarters

building in New York City.
<ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#20:1> The building is owned by Rock-McGraw, Inc., a corporation in which

the Registrant and Rockefeller Group, Inc. are the sole shareholders.
<ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#21:1> The Registrant occupies approximately 941,000 square feet of the

rentable space under a 30-year lease which includes renewal options for two

additional 15-year periods.
<ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#22:1> In addition, the Registrant subleases for its own account

approximately 694,000 square feet of space for periods up to 25 years.</p>

<p> <ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#23:1> The largest complex owned by the Registrant is located in

Hightstown, NJ which houses the offices for accounting operations, data

processing services, other service departments and a warehouse.
<ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#24:1> The Registrant has consolidated its domestic book distribution

operations by consolidating the distribution operations in Blue Ridge Summit,

PA and Hightstown, NJ to Westerville and Blacklick, OH.
<ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#25:1> The warehouse in Hightstown, NJ is leased to a tenant.
<ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#26:1> The warehouse in Blue Ridge Summit, PA is vacant.</p>

<p> <ICE-USA:W1B-030:W1B-030#27:1> While the Registrant and its subsidiaries are defendants in

numerous legal proceedings in the United States and abroad, neither the

Registrant nor its subsidiaries are a party to, nor are any of their

properties subject to, any known material pending legal proceedings which

Registrant believes will result in a material adverse effect on Registrant 's

financial statements or business operations.</p>
