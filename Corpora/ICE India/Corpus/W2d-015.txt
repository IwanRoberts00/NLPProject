<h> <bold> SPICY PINEAPPLE PUNCH </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#7:1>
( A lovely drink to welcome your guests, serve it warm before
lunch, especially at Christmas time, or enjoy it chilled with the barbecue
during the long, warm summer evenings.) </p>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#8:1>
<bold> <h> Serves: Four </h>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#9:1>
Time required: 35 minutes

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#10:1>
225 ml. water

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#11:1>
500 ml. pineapple juice

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#12:1>
12.5 cm. cinnamon, broken into pieces

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#13:1>
6 cloves

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#14:1>
6 green cardamoms, bruised

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#15:1>
1 1/2 tsp. ( 7 g.) fresh mint leaves, chopped

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#16:1>
90 ml. brandy </bold>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#17:1>
PUT the water, half the pineapple juice, cinnamon, cloves,
cardamom and mint into a saucepan.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#18:1>
Bring to the boil, cover the pan and simmer gently for 20 minutes.
</p>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#19:1>
Remove from the heat and allow to cool.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#20:1>
Keep the pan covered. </p>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#21:1>
Strain the drink and add the remaining pineapple juice and the
brandy.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#22:1>
Mix well. </p>

<h> <bold> MEAT <indig> SAMOSAS </indig> </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#31:1>
( The ever popular <indig> samosas </indig> make a wonderful
treat on any occasion.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#32:1>
In India, <indig> samosas </indig> are a familiar sight at wedding
receptions and cocktail parties.) </p>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#33:1>
<bold> Makes: 16 <indig> samosas </indig>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#34:1>
Time required: One hour 10 minutes plus cooling time

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#35:1>
For the filling:

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#36:1>
2 tbsp. ( 30 ml.) oil

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#37:1>
2 ( 110 g.) onions, chopped fine

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#38:1>
225 g. lean lamb or beef mince

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#39:1>
4 flakes ( 8 g.) garlic, crushed

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#40:1>
1.5 cm. ginger, grated fine

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#41:1>
1/2 tsp. ( 2 g.) turmeric powder

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#42:1>
2 tsp ( 10 g.) coriander powder

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#43:1>
1 1/2 tsp. ( 7 g.) cummin powder

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#44:1>
1 tsp. ( 5 g.) chilli powder

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#45:1>
1/2 tsp. ( 2 g.) salt or to taste

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#46:1>
125 ml. warm water

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#47:1>
150 g. frozen green peas

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#48:1>
2 tbsp. ( 30 g.) desiccated coconut

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#49:1>
1 tsp. ( 5 g.) garam masala powder

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#50:1>
2 green chillies, chopped fine ( deseeded if a milder falvour is
preferred)

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#51:1>
20 g. coriander leaves, chopped

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#52:1>
1 tbsp. ( 15 ml.) lemon juice

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#53:1>
For the pastry ( for the covering):

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#54:1>
225 g. flour

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#55:1>
50 g. ghee or butter

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#56:1>
1/2 tsp. ( 2 g.) salt

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#57:1>
75 ml. warm water

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#58:1>
oil for deep frying

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#59:1>
To prepare the filling: </bold>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#60:1>
HEAT the oil over medium heat and fry the onions till light
brown.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#61:1>
Add the mince, garlic and ginger.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#62:1>
Stir-fry over low heat until almost dry.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#63:1>
Add the turmeric powder, coriander powder, cummin powder, chilli
powder and salt.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#64:1>
Stir-fry until the mince is lightly browned.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#65:1>
Add the water and the green peas and bring to the boil.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#66:1>
Cover and simmer for 25 to 30 minutes.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#67:1>
If there is any liquid left, remove the lid and cook the mince over
medium heat until it is completely dry, stirring frequently.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#68:1>
Stir in the desiccated coconut, garam masala powder, green chillies
and coriander leaves. </p>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#69:1>
Remove from heat and add the lemon juice.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#70:1>
Cool thoroughly before filling the <indig> samosas </indig> . </p>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#71:1>
<h> <bold> To prepare the pastry: </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#72:1>
Add the butter and salt to the flour.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#73:1>
Rub in well.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#74:1>
Add enough warm water to mix a soft dough.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#75:1>
Knead until the dough feels soft and velvety to the touch.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#76:1>
Divide the dough into eight portions and roll into 10 cm. discs.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#77:1>
Cut each into two.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#78:1>
Roll each semicircle of the pastry to make an envelope.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#79:1>
Moisten the straight edge with a little warm water.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#80:1>
Fold the semicircle of the pastry in half to form a triangular cone.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#81:1>
Join the straight edges by pressing them hard into each other.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#82:1>
Make sure that there are no gaps. </p>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#83:1>
Fill these cones with the prepared filling, leaving about 1/2 cm.
border on the top of the cone.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#84:1>
Now moisten the top edges and press them hard together. </p>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#85:1>
Deep fry the <indig> samosas </indig> in hot oil over gentle heat
until they are golden brown <O> <&> page </&> 80 <*> black square</*> FEMINA
JUNE 1, 1997 <*> BLACK SQUARE</*> </O> and drain on absorbent paper. </p>

<h> <bold>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#97:1>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#98:1>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#99:1>
700 g. chicken breast, skinned and deboned

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#100:1>
1 tsp. ( 5 g.) salt or to taste

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#101:1>
3 flakes ( 6 g.) garlic, coarsely chopped

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#102:1>
2 tbsp. ( 30 ml.) oil

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#103:1>
1 1/2 tsp. ( 7 g.) coriander powder

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#104:1>
1/4 tsp. ( 1 g.) turmeric powder

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#105:1>
3/4 tsp. ( 4 g.) chilli powder

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#112:1>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#113:1>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#114:1>
Add coriander powder, turmeric powder and chilli powder.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#115:1>
Fry for four minutes, stirring frequently.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#116:1>
Remove from heat and stir in the lemon juice and chopped coriander
leaves. </p>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#117:1>
Serve as a starter, garnished with crisp lettuce leaves, sliced
cucumber, raw sliced onion and wedges of lemon or with a selection of <indig>
chutneys </indig> .

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#118:1>
Chicken <indig> chaat </indig> can also be served with drinks on
cocktail sticks, hot or cold. </p> <bold>

<h> <bold> CHICKEN LIVER <indig> MASALA </indig> </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#127:1>
( Curried liver, quite popular with the Muslims in India, is
made more interesting by adding diced potatoes and frozen green peas.) </p>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#128:1>
<bold> <h> Serves: Four </h>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#129:1>
Time required: 55 minutes

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#130:1>
450 g. chicken liver

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#131:1>
4 tbsp. ( 60 ml.) oil

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#132:1>
1 ( 70 g.) onion, chopped fine

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#133:1>
5 cm. cinnamon, broken into pieces

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#134:1>
225 g. potatoes, peeled and diced

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#135:1>
1 1/4 tsp. ( 6 g.) salt or to taste

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#136:1>
90 ml. warm water

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#137:1>
4 flakes ( 8 g.) garlic, crushed

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#138:1>
1 small tin of tomatoes

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#139:1>
100 g. frozen green peas

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#140:1>
3 green chillies

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#141:1>
1/2 tsp. ( 2 g.) <indig> garam masala </indig> powder

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#142:1>
Mix to a paste with two tsp. ( 10 ml.) water.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#143:1>
2 tsp. ( 10 g.) coriander powder

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#144:1>
1 tsp. ( 5 g.) cummin powder

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#145:1>
1 tsp. ( 5 g.) turmeric powder

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#146:1>
1/2 tsp. ( 2 g.) chilli powder </bold>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#147:1>
CLEAN the liver, remove all the skin and gristle, and cut
roughly into 1.5 cm. pieces.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#148:1>
Heat two tbsp. oil over medium heat and fry the onions and cinnamon
until the onions are soft.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#149:1>
Add the potatoes and 1/4 tsp. ( 1 g.) salt and stirfry the
potatoes for about two minutes.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#150:1>
Add the water.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#151:1>
Cover the pan and simmer until the potatoes are tender. </p>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#152:1>
Heat the remaining oil over medium heat in a heavy bottomed wide
pan.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#153:1>
A non stick or cast iron pan is ideal as the liver needs to be
stir-fried over high heat.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#154:1>
Add the garlic and stir-fry for 30 seconds.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#155:1>
Add the spice paste, lower the heat and stir-fry for about two
minutes. </p> <O> <*> black square</*> FEMINA JUNE 1, 1997 <*> BLACK SQUARE

</O>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#156:1>
Add half of the tomatoes, along with some of the juice, stir and
cook for a further two to three minutes, breaking the tomatoes with the
spoon. </p>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#157:1>
When the mixture is fairly dry, add the liver and adjust heat to
medium high.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#158:1>
Stir-fry the liver for three to four minutes.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#159:1>
Add the remaining tomatoes and the juice, stir-fry for five to six
minutes. </p>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#160:1>
Cover the pan and simmer for about eight minutes.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#161:1>
Add the potatoes, green peas, green chillies and the remaining salt

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#162:1>
Adjust heat to medium and cook, uncovered, for further five minutes.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#163:1>
Stir in the <indig> garam masala </indig> powder and remove from
heat. </p>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#164:1>
Serve with plain fried rice or <indig> chapaties </indig> or
<indig> rotis </indig> and <indig> saagwala dal </indig> . </p>

<h> <bold> <indig> KHEEMA MATTER </indig> </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#173:1>
( Lean mince is combined with garden peas and ground almonds and

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#174:1>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#175:1>
Time required: 55 minutes

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#176:1>
6 tbsp. ( 90 ml.) oil

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#177:1>
1 tsp. ( 5 g.) cummin seeds

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#178:1>
2 red chillies

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#179:1>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#181:1>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#182:1>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#183:1>
1/2 tsp. ( 2 g.) turmeric powder

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#184:1>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#185:1>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#186:1>
1/2 tsp. ( 2 g.) chilli powder

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#187:1>
1 small tin of tomatoes or 3-4 ( 200 g.) fresh tomatoes, skinned and

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#188:1>
1 tsp. ( 5 g.) salt or to taste

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#190:1>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#191:1>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#193:1>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#194:1>
1/2 tsp. ( 2 g.) <indig> garam masala </indig> powder

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#195:1>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#196:1>
25 g. coriander leaves, chopped </bold>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#198:1>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#199:1>
Stir and cook until the mince is evenly browned. </p>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#200:1>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#201:1>

Add the tomatoes along with all the juice, and saute for four

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#205:1>
Add the mince and cook for eight minutes, stirring frequently.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#206:1>
Add the salt and water and stir in the curd.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#207:1>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#208:1>
Cover the pan and simmer for 20 minutes. </p>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#209:1>
Add the frozen peas and simmer for a further 10 minutes.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#210:1>
If using fresh peas, boil until tender before adding to the mince.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#211:1>
Stir in the ground almonds and simmer for two minutes.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#212:1>
Remove from heat and stir in the <indig> garam masala </indig>
powder. </p>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#213:1>
Transfer onto a serving dish and arrange the sliced eggs on top.

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#214:1>
Garnish with the coriander leaves. </p>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#215:1>
Serve with <indig> chapatis, parathas </indig> and/ or fried
brown rice and potato <indig> raita </indig> . </p>

<h> <bold> GREEN BEANS IN GARLIC BUTTER </bold> </h>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#224:1>
( Tender green beans, cooked in their own juice, make a
mouth-watering accompaniment to any rich meat, fish or poultry dish.) </p>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#225:1>
<bold> <h> Serves: Four </h>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#226:1>
Time required: 15 minutes

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#227:1>
25 g. unsalted butter <O> <&> page </&> 82 <*> black square</*>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#228:1>
1/2 tsp. ( 2 g.) cummin seeds

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#229:1>
4 flakes ( 8 g.) garlic, crushed or finely chopped

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#230:1>
1/2 tsp. ( 2 g.) chilli powder

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#231:1>
450 g. frozen whole green beans

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#232:1>
1/2 tsp. ( 2 g.) salt or to taste </bold>

<p> 
<ICE-IND:W2D-015#233:1>

<ICE-IND:W2D-015#234:1>
